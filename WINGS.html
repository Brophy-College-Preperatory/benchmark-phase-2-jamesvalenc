Brandon Ingram.

Brandon Ingram is one of the most underrated players in the NBA because of his ability to impact the game on both ends of the court. As a versatile scorer, he combines a smooth mid-range game with excellent finishing at the rim and a reliable three-point shot, making him a nightmare for defenders. Despite his consistent offensive output, Ingram often flies under the radar due to playing in a smaller market and sharing the spotlight with other stars. Additionally, his improved playmaking and defensive contributions show that he’s not just a scorer but a well-rounded player who elevates his team. Ingram’s talent and work ethic make him deserving of more recognition as one of the league’s most complete and impactful forwards.

CJ Mcollum.

CJ McCollum consistently produces elite scoring numbers without receiving the recognition he deserves. Over his career, McCollum has averaged nearly 20 points per game, including a career-high 23.1 points per game in the 2020-21 season, while shooting an impressive 39.5% from three-point range. Beyond scoring, he’s also an underrated playmaker, averaging over 5 assists per game during the 2023-24 season with the New Orleans Pelicans. Despite these numbers, McCollum is often overshadowed by bigger names in the league, even though he’s one of only 10 players to average 20+ points and 5+ assists while shooting 38% or better from three over multiple seasons. His consistent efficiency and versatility as a scorer, facilitator, and leader make him an invaluable player who deserves more recognition.
